Feathered Collisions: Bird Species and Total Hits in the Sky

Up in the sky, a sad story is happening. Birds, our sky buddies, are getting hurt by airplanes. It's not just a few; it's at least 86 species of animals being killed, and maybe more. Some birds get so torn apart, such that we can't even tell what kind they were.

Look at the chart. Each bar isn't just a number; it's a bird's life, cut short while flying. This isn't about vegan stuff or climate talk — it's about feeling for our flying friends. The sky used to be a safe place for birds, but now, it's like a war zone.

Who's to blame? Surprisingly, it's the companies that fly us around. They're supposed to be cool inventions, but they're also causing harm to nature. Our fast and easy flights shouldn't mean sadness for the birds.

This isn't just another meaningless chart. It's proof we need to change, a request to care more, and a reminder that progress shouldn't hurt the ones who share the sky with us. The birds may be quiet now, but their story speaks loudly — asking us to think twice about how we travel through their home in the sky.

JUST STOP PLANES

Just stop planes is a non-profit organization that aims to stop planes from flying. We believe that planes are the main cause of global warming and we want to stop them from flying. We are a group of people who are passionate about the environment and we want to make a change. We are not funded by any government or organization. We are funded by people like you who want to make a change. If you want to help us, please donate to our cause.
